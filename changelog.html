Changelog

What has changed on the site, and when. Updated as the platform grows.

A running record of what changes on BioNexus Hub, so you can see the site is maintained and current. Newest first.

June 2026

  • Beginner rebuild of the foundations. Python, R, and the command-line Level 1 lessons were rewritten from zero for people with no coding background, with diagrams and a plain-English definition for every term.
  • A Level 2 for each language. New Intermediate lessons for Python, R, and the command line, with worked-solution exercises.
  • Scored lesson checks. Every lesson's quiz now adds up to a "Lesson check" with a 70% pass mark, so you can tell whether the ideas landed.
  • Self-checking exercises. The Python practice exercises now tell you when your own answer is correct, and the R and command-line lessons show the exact output you should expect.
  • Real data, not just rehearsals. The variant-calling lesson now walks through a real E. coli run from the ENA, and the RNA-seq flagship runs the real airway dataset end to end, each with a note on what real data breaks that the toy examples hid.
  • Privacy policy. A clear, plain-language privacy policy covering what little data the site touches.
  • Accuracy pass. Corrected the DESeq2 normalization wording (it also corrects for library composition), added duplicate marking and indel normalization to the variant pipeline, and relabeled "runs in your browser" to "runs free in Colab" where the code runs in Colab rather than in the page.
  • More visuals. Diagrams added across the foundation lessons, by request, so concepts are shown, not just described.

Earlier

  • Built the full roadmap: Foundations plus Tracks on data handling, NGS, RNA-seq, RNA biology, statistics, and reproducibility and career skills.
  • Added in-browser runnable Python cells, a sequence-alignment and BLAST mini-course with a graded final assessment, printable-free cheat sheets, a glossary, and a beginner Help and FAQ.

Spotted something out of date or wrong? Use the feedback button on any page, it reaches us directly.
